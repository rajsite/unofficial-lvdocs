Estimating Power and Frequency

If a frequency component is between two frequency lines, the frequency component appears as energy spread among adjacent frequency lines with reduced amplitude. The actual peak is between the two frequency lines. Also, if two or more frequency peaks are within six lines of each other, they contribute to inflating the estimated powers and skewing the actual frequencies. You can reduce this effect by decreasing the number of lines spanned by the previous equation. If two peaks are within six lines of each other, it is likely that they are already interfering with one another because of spectral leakage.

If you want the total power in a given frequency range, sum the power in each bin included in the frequency range and divide by the noise power bandwidth of the smoothing window.
