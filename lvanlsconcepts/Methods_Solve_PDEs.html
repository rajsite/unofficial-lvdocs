Solving PDEs with Numerical Methods

You can solve a partial differential equation (PDE) analytically in many ways. For example, the separation of variables method solves several types of PDE. This method separates the equation into several ODEs. The solution to the PDE is the product of the solutions of the separated ODEs.

In practical applications, it is impossible to know the analytical solution of the equation. In these cases, you can use numerical methods to solve PDEs. Unlike analytical methods that return the exact solution, numerical methods approximate the solution. The finite difference method and finite element method are two of the most widely used numerical methods in scientific computing.
