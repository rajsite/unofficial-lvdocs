Dirichlet Condition

The Dirichlet condition specifies the values of unknown function u on the boundary of the domain. Numerically, the Dirichlet condition guarantees the existence and uniqueness of the PDE. The Dirichlet condition is sometimes a constant, which means the values of u do not change on the boundary. For example, a 1D wave equation with a constant boundary condition describes the motion of a string with fixed ends.
