Domain

The domain specifies where the PDE applies in time and spatial variables. In practical applications, you often define a PDE in practical applications on a bounded domain, which can be a line, rectangle, polygon, or another geometric area or body.

For example, consider a 2D heat equation, which describes the thermal distribution in a plate over time. The plate is the domain of the heat equation. You cannot use the same equation to describe the heat diffusion problem at a location outside of the plate because the media is different.

You also can specify an unbounded domain, such as with a wave propagation problem over the half plane. The unbounded domain can be either infinite or semi-infinite.
