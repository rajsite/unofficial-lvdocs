Types of PDEs

A partial differential equation (PDE) is an equation that contains partial derivatives and at least two independent variables. In general, a PDE is written as

where u is the unknown function and depends on all other variables, t denotes the time variable, and x, y, and so on denote the spatial variables. Although x, y, and so on are the Cartesian coordinates, the equations can be defined on other coordinate systems, such as the polar coordinate system. f can be a constant or function that depends on time or spatial variables.
