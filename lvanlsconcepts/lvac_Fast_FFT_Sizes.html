Fast FFT Sizes

When the size of the input sequence is a power of two, N = 2m, you can implement the computation of the discrete Fourier transform (DFT) with approximately Nlog2(N) operations, which makes the calculation of the DFT much faster. DSP literature refers to the algorithms for faster DFT calculation as fast Fourier transforms (FFTs). Common input sequence sizes that are a power of two include 512, 1,024, and 2,048.

When the size of the input sequence is not a power of two but is factorable as the product of small prime numbers, you can use a mixed radix Cooley-Tukey algorithm to efficiently compute the DFT of the input sequence.
