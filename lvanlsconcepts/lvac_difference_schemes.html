The Difference Schemes

Suppose you know that the value of function f(x) is evaluated on the uniform points {x0, x1, …, xn}.

You can approximate the derivative of f(x) on the ith point by the following difference schemes. Let h denote the interval between two adjacent points.

Backward
First order central
Second order central
Note   You can extend these difference schemes to the partial derivative when f depends on two or more variables.

The accuracy of the difference scheme relies on interval h. The smaller interval always results in more accurate approximation.
