The Differentiation Index

It is impossible to solve the derivatives of an unknown function directly from differential algebraic equations (DAEs). However, DAEs can determine an explicit form of the derivatives by differentiating between the original equations. Then, you can reformulate original DAEs to equivalent ODEs.

When you differentiate between the original equations, you create a differentiation index. The differentiation index is the minimum differential equations that are required to solve the unknown derivatives.

The differentiation index is often used to measure the difficulty in theoretical analysis and numerical solutions for DAEs. The higher the differentiation index, the more difficult it is to solve the DAEs numerically.
