The heat equation often describes thermal distribution in a bar or a plate over time. The non-zero function on the right side of the equation shows that there is an external heat source that might or might not depend on time.

The coefficient k in the heat and wave equations depends on physical parameters. k relates to diffusion rate, mass density, or other physical properties of the medium where heat diffusion or wave motion occurs. When the equation is not homogeneous, k can be a non-constant, making the equation no longer linear.
