The wave equation describes the wave motion that arises in elasticity and acoustics. If an external force acts on the motion, the right side of the equation is non-zero. For example, the 1D wave equation can describe the motion of a string as time progresses. If you take gravity into consideration, the right side should be -g, where g is the gravitational acceleration.

The coefficient k in the heat and wave equations depends on physical parameters. k relates to diffusion rate, mass density, or other physical properties of the medium where wave motion happens. When the equation is not homogeneous, k can be a non-constant, making the equation no longer linear.

Note   The heat and wave equations are evolutionary equations. The heat equation contains the first order derivative of t, and the wave equation contains the second order derivative of t.
