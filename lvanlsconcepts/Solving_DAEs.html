Solving Differential Algebraic Equations

Differential algebraic equations (DAEs) contain an unknown function and the derivatives of that function. The DAE is one kind of differential equation in the implicit form F(t, x, x') = 0 where x' is the derivative of x with respect to t. t is known as the time variable. x and x' are known as the state and state derivative, respectively.

If x' can be solved directly from the implicit form, such as x' = G(t, x) then the original equation becomes an ordinary differential equation (ODE). Therefore, ODE can be considered as a special instance of DAE.
